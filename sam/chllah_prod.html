What is Challah Bread?

Challah bread is a traditional braided Jewish bread. This egg-bread is characterized by its three-braided form, tender texture and cottony strands.

Variations of Shome's challah bread include:

  1. Traditional Challah
  2. Challah rolls
  3. Round challah
  4. Key shaped challah

The challah bread can be eaten with or without toppings. Common toppings for this type of bread include sesame seeds, nuts, candied fruits, chocolate, seeds and herbs.

Origin

Challah bread is traditionally a Jewish bakery product of Eastern European origin, commonly consumed during shabbat, weddings and religious holidays. The meaning of the word Challah is thought to mean a “piece of dough removed from loaf as an offering”.

In 1488, the first recorded mention of Challah as the sabbath bread was made. It was introduced to the United States in the 19th century by Jewish immigrants. Today, challah bread is a well-known bread type that is consumed outside of Jewish circles and is produced in a variety of bakeries.
